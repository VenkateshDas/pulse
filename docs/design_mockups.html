HALO Design System — “a cockpit, not a dashboard.” Futuristic command center: bold, glowing, but every pixel legible and every signal actionable.

00HALO Design System

Dark-first. One electric accent (Ion Cyan) for live data and primary action; violet for history/secondary; the three safety colors are reserved exclusively for safety meaning — never decoration. Numbers always monospaced. Glow = “live and healthy”; absence of glow = inert.

07Interaction & motion rules

Motion

Springs only (matched to macOS), 150–300ms. Treemap zoom = continuous transform, never crossfade. Live charts scroll, never repaint. Glow pulses reserved for: live indicators, the timeline spike, and freed-space confirmation. Nothing else animates idly.

Keyboard layer

⌘K command palette everywhere (“clean safe”, “empty trash”, “why is cpu hot”). 1–7 switch sections. space toggle selection. TUI heritage, prosumer love.

The control feeling

Every screen answers, in order: Am I OK? → What changed? → What can I do? Status top-left, deltas everywhere (−4.2 G/wk), action bottom-right glowing. The user always leaves a screen having been offered a verb.

Verification ritual

After every clean: counter animates from old free-space to new, sourced from the real df reading — the mac-monitor lesson (“why didn’t free space change?”) turned into the product’s signature moment of proof.
